    Chicken Curry with Mustard and Onion¶

    This recipe is optimized for 750g of chicken, focusing on the specific sequence of emulsifying the curd and spices to prevent splitting.

    This is the first recipe that I learnt under the guidance of my dear friend Sayan (he is a great cook) and Google Gemini.

    Ingredients

    • Protein: 750g Chicken (bone-in preferred for flavor)
    • Fat: 4-5 tbsp Mustard Oil (heated to smoking point)
    • Aromatics: 3 Large onions (thinly sliced), 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste
    • Dairy: 150g Whisked Curd (Dahi) (the onions and the curd form the base of the curry)
    • Whole Spices: 2 Bay leaves, 4 Cardamom pods, 1-inch Cinnamon stick
    • Ground Spices: 1 tsp Turmeric, 2 tsp Kashmiri Red Chilli powder, 1.5 tsp Coriander powder, 1 tsp Cumin powder, 1 tsp Garam Masala

    The Macros for this is great and I can generally eat this in 3 servings. Sometimes with rice or roti or just raw.

    • Calories 2,135
    • Protein 155g
    • Fats 139g
    • Carbs 59g

    Technical Procedure¶

    1. The Tempering (Fat Preparation)¶

    Heat the mustard oil in a heavy-bottomed pan until it reaches its smoking point to remove the raw, pungent edge. Lower the heat and add the whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cinnamon). Once they sizzle and release their oils, add the sliced onions.

    2. The Maillard Reaction (Onion Base)¶

    Saut√© the onions with a pinch of salt over medium-high heat. You are looking for a deep golden brown (Beresta stage) rather than just translucent. This provides the foundational sweetness and color of the curry.

    3. The Ginger-Garlic & Spice Layering¶

    Add the ginger-garlic paste. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell evaporates.

    Note: If the pan is too dry, add a splash of water to prevent the garlic from burning, which introduces bitterness.

    4. The Curd-Spice Emulsion (Critical Step)¶

    In a separate bowl, whisk the curd with the turmeric, red chilli, coriander, and cumin powders.

    Lower the stove heat to minimum.

Gradually stir in the curd-spice mixture.

Mechanism: Whisking the spices directly into the curd stabilizes the proteins, making it less likely to curdle when it hits the heat.

Stir continuously until the oil (fat) starts to separate from the masala (the "bhuna" stage).

    5. Protein Integration¶

    Add the chicken pieces. Increase the heat to medium-high and sear the meat with the masala for 5-7 minutes. This coats the chicken and ensures the juices are locked in.

    6. Simmer and Finish¶

    Add approximately 200ml of hot water (using hot water maintains the temperature of the fats). Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes.

    Optional: If you want to include potatoes as we discussed, par-fry them in mustard oil beforehand and add them at this stage.

Finish with garam masala and a teaspoon of raw mustard oil or ghee for an aromatic punch.
